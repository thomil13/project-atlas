If a friend or family member is affected

Thank you for stepping up. I know it is not easy to see someone you care about suffer, especially when it's from something invisible, like mental health issues. Helping someone who's battling with these issues can be taxing, but there are a number of things that you can do.

Take Time for them - It isn't easy for someone to talk about their issues. So let them take their time, and don't push them. You might be the very first person they're opening up to about their issues.

Be supportive - Acknowledge their feelings sand thoughts, they're real to the person expressing them. When talking to them, ask open-ended questions, and let them know that they can say as much or as little as they want.

Know your limits - You're not Atlas, you don't have to carry the weight of the heavens on your shoulders. More importantly, you're likely not a trained therapist either. So make sure you look after yourself as well, and encourage your friend or family member to reach out to a professional.
