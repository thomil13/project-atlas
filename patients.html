If you're affected

First of all, I'm grateful that you're here, that you're alive. It's not easy dealing with an issue that is invisible to everyone except you. I know, I've been where you are, I know how it feels when your own mind turns against you. But there is help available, genuine help. Reaching out and asking for help might be scary, I've personally struggled with that for as long as I can remember. But it can also be a massive relief to finally talk about things with someone else, whether that's a friend, family member, or a mental health professional. This is not a sign of weakness, it is a sign of immense strength! I've gathered together a few links below, including one to a service called MyMind, the service I used to find my therapist. I've also added a few tips based on my personal experience.

You're worth being helped - It is important to remember this. Our mind can be deceitful and try to tell us that we're just a burden. We're not. You're not! Always remember that!

You're not alone - The ancient greek titan Atlas was condemned to carry the weight of the heavens on his shoulders. But you're not Atlas! You don't have to bear your load alone. There are people out there who appreciate you and who'll gladly help you!

You're always making progress - When the days all fade into one, and everything is drab and grey, it might feel like you're standing still and life is passing you by. But every day, we make progress. Be it going to work or school despite dreading it, or even just waking up and sitting up in bed. Every little step counts, be it big or small.

MyMind

Provids affordable and accessible therapy and counselling options across Ireland, both in-person and online.

Visit MyMind
Grow Mental Health

Grow provides group-based peer support groups throughout Ireland for those suffering from mental health issues.

Visit Grow Mental Health
turn2me

An online mental health service that provides individual and group-based support for people struggling with their mental health.

Visit turn2me

Emergency Contacts

If you feel like you, or someone you care about, are in immediate danger of harming themselves or others, please reach out to one of the numbers below!

Call Emergency Services!

If you or some you know is at risk of physical harm, please call 112 or 999 immediately!

Samaritans Ireland

If you need immediate help, call Samaritans on 116 123, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year!
